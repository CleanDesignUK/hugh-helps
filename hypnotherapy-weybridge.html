Hypnosis itself is not dangerous. It is a gentle therapeutic technique that promotes a calm, focused state of mind. By combining relaxation with an inward focus, hypnosis can help reduce stress, discomfort, and even issues like anxiety or chronic pain. It also tends to boost confidence, self-control, and emotional well-being.

Recognised as a valid therapeutic modality by the British Medical Association since 1955, hypnotherapy is safe when practised by a qualified, insured professional. I follow these guidelines to ensure you receive a secure, supportive experience.

Hypnosis does not put you to sleep. You’ll feel relaxed yet very much aware of what is happening. If, for instance, a fire alarm were to sound, you would notice it straight away. Many people liken hypnosis to a vivid daydream or a meditation-like state. You remain in control and can open your eyes or speak at any time.

Most people can experience hypnosis if they choose to and feel comfortable with it. Everyone’s level of responsiveness varies, but with practice and a willingness to engage, virtually anyone can benefit from hypnotherapy.

Hypnosis is not mind-control; it is a collaborative process in which you retain complete autonomy.

Hypnosis is not a truth serum. You remain aware of what you’re saying and can choose what you share. Hypnotherapy is designed to support you in achieving your therapeutic goals, not to force you to divulge personal information unless you wish to do so.
